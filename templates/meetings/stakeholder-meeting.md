## Meeting Types & Templates

### Status Update Meeting

**Focus:** Share progress, align on status  
**Frequency:** Weekly/Bi-weekly  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Key Sections:**
- Progress update
- Metrics
- Blockers
- Next steps

---

### Decision-Making Meeting

**Focus:** Make specific decisions  
**Frequency:** As needed  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Key Sections:**
- Context
- Options analysis
- Recommendation
- Decision
- Action items

---

### Planning Meeting

**Focus:** Plan upcoming work  
**Frequency:** Quarterly/Monthly  
**Duration:** 1-2 hours  
**Key Sections:**
- Goals and objectives
- Proposed initiatives
- Resource requirements
- Timeline
- Commitments

---

### Review/Demo Meeting

**Focus:** Show completed work  
**Frequency:** End of sprint/milestone  
**Duration:** 1 hour  
**Key Sections:**
- Demo
- Metrics/results
- Feedback
- Next steps

## Stakeholder Communication Best Practices

### Before the Meeting

**Preparation:**
- [ ] Send agenda 24-48 hours in advance
- [ ] Share relevant documents/data
- [ ] Identify decisions needed
- [ ] Prepare visual aids (slides, demos)
- [ ] Test technology (screen share, video)
- [ ] Schedule appropriate duration
- [ ] Confirm attendance

**Materials to Prepare:**
- Executive summary (1-pager)
- Detailed metrics/data
- Options analysis for decisions
- Timeline/roadmap visual
- Demo if applicable

---

### During the Meeting

**DO:**
‚úÖ Start on time  
‚úÖ State objective upfront  
‚úÖ Use visuals  
‚úÖ Focus on business impact  
‚úÖ Be direct about challenges  
‚úÖ Ask for specific decisions  
‚úÖ Take notes  
‚úÖ Manage time  
‚úÖ Summarize decisions/actions at end  

**DON'T:**
‚ùå Read slides verbatim  
‚ùå Get defensive  
‚ùå Use too much jargon  
‚ùå Surprise people with problems  
‚ùå Leave without decisions/actions  
‚ùå Go over time  
‚ùå Dominate the conversation

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Pushback on Timeline

**Response:**
1. Acknowledge concerns
2. Explain constraints
3. Show data/rationale
4. Offer options:
   - Reduce scope
   - Add resources
   - Accept delay
5. Get decision on trade-offs

---

### Conflicting Stakeholder Priorities

**Response:**
1. Clarify each stakeholder's goals
2. Show impact of each option
3. Use data to inform decision
4. Escalate if needed
5. Document final decision clearly

---

### Unexpected Bad News

**Response:**
1. Be direct and honest
2. Provide context
3. Explain impact
4. Present mitigation plan
5. State what you need
6. Commit to follow-up timeline

---

## Executive Stakeholder Tips

**Key Principles:**
- Lead with business impact
- Be concise
- Use data
- Present solutions, not just problems
- Know your numbers
- Anticipate questions
- Have backup plans ready

**Executive Attention Span:**
- First 30 seconds: Critical
- First 5 minutes: Hook them or lose them
- Recommendations upfront: They appreciate it

**What Executives Care About:**
- Revenue impact
- User/customer impact
- Risk mitigation
- Competitive advantage
- Resource efficiency
- Strategic alignment
